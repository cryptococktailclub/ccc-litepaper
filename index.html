Crypto Cocktail Club

Ownership Infrastructure for Cocktail Culture

Designed for bars, not traders.

Crypto Cocktail Club is building ownership infrastructure for cocktail culture — aligning creators, venues, and members through on-chain access, real-world redemption, and programmable incentives.

The Problem

Cocktail culture generates enduring economic and cultural value, yet the people who create it rarely participate in the upside. Recipes circulate freely. Venues profit episodically. Loyalty rewards consumption, not contribution.

Mission

Crypto Cocktail Club exists to realign cocktail culture so creators, venues, and members all participate in its value — transparently, sustainably, and without speculation-first incentives.

Strategy

  • Membership NFTs — Scarce access passes with evolving utility
  • Redeemable Cocktail IP — On-chain recipes with real-world use
  • Venue Network — Bars monetize culture beyond the four walls

Access as Infrastructure

Membership NFTs are not collectibles. They are programmable access credentials that gate experiences, redemption rights, and future protocol participation.

Membership Tiers

Base Access, drops, community participation
Gold Priority access, partner perks
Platinum Governance influence and protocol participation

TIPZ Token

Incentive coordination layer

TIPZ is the incentive and settlement mechanism that aligns creators, venues, and members inside the Crypto Cocktail Club protocol.

TIPZ is earned through participation — by contributing recipes, hosting redemptions, supporting venues, or engaging with protocol experiences.

The token exists to reward real activity and to coordinate value flow between off-chain hospitality and on-chain infrastructure.

TIPZ is not positioned as a speculative asset or consumer payment rail. It is designed to function quietly in the background, reinforcing behavior rather than driving attention.

Why TIPZ Is Not a Payment Token

  • Bars already operate on mature payment rails.
  • Volatile consumer payments create operational risk.
  • Hospitality margins do not tolerate speculation.

TIPZ is designed to coordinate incentives, not replace POS systems. Settlement into stable rails can occur off-chain where appropriate, preserving operational simplicity for venues.

Founding Thesis

Cocktail culture is built by individuals — bartenders, creators, and operators — yet ownership of that culture has historically accrued elsewhere.

Recipes travel faster than the people who create them. Venues profit from differentiation. Guests participate in culture without participating in its upside.

Crypto Cocktail Club exists to introduce ownership primitives to hospitality — without disrupting what already works.

By treating cocktails as intellectual property, experiences as access-controlled systems, and venues as network participants, CCC reframes nightlife as infrastructure.
