Crypto Cocktail Club

Ownership Infrastructure for Cocktail Culture

Designed for bars, not traders.

Crypto Cocktail Club aligns creators, venues, and members through access-controlled experiences, real-world redemption, and on-chain incentives.

The Problem

Cocktail culture generates enduring economic and cultural value, yet the people who create it rarely participate in the upside. Recipes circulate freely. Venues profit episodically. Loyalty systems reward consumption, not contribution.

Mission

Crypto Cocktail Club exists to realign cocktail culture so creators, venues, and members all participate in its value — transparently, sustainably, and without speculation-first incentives.

Who This Is For

  • Bartenders & Creators — who generate culture but rarely retain ownership
  • Independent Venues — seeking new revenue without operational burden
  • Members — who want access, participation, and alignment—not speculation

Crypto Cocktail Club is not designed for traders, yield farmers, or extractive intermediaries. It is designed for the people who already make nightlife work.

Strategy

  • Membership NFTs — Scarce access passes with evolving utility
  • Redeemable Cocktail IP — On-chain recipes with real-world use
  • Venue Network — Bars monetize culture beyond the four walls

Access as Infrastructure

Membership NFTs are not collectibles. They are programmable access credentials that gate experiences, redemption rights, and future protocol participation.

Membership Tiers

Base Protocol access, community participation, and standard drops
Gold Priority access, partner venue privileges, enhanced redemption rights
Platinum Governance surfaces, protocol influence, and long-term alignment

TIPZ Token

Incentive coordination layer

TIPZ is the incentive and settlement mechanism that aligns creators, venues, and members inside the Crypto Cocktail Club protocol.

TIPZ is earned through participation — contributing recipes, hosting redemptions, supporting venues, or engaging with protocol experiences.

  • Creators & Bartenders — 30%
  • Venue Network — 25%
  • Member Incentives — 20%
  • Protocol Treasury — 25%

TIPZ is not positioned as a speculative asset or consumer payment rail. It operates quietly in the background, reinforcing behavior rather than driving attention.

Why TIPZ Is Not a Payment Token

  • Bars already operate on mature payment rails
  • Volatile consumer payments create operational risk
  • Hospitality margins do not tolerate speculation

TIPZ coordinates incentives rather than replacing POS systems. Settlement into stable rails can occur off-chain, preserving simplicity for venues.

What Crypto Cocktail Club Is Not

  • Not a drink NFT marketplace
  • Not a DAO-first governance experiment
  • Not a consumer payment token
  • Not a loyalty points rebrand

CCC is infrastructure that sits beneath hospitality, quietly coordinating access, ownership, and incentive flow without asking venues or guests to change how they behave.

How TIPZ Circulates

TIPZ enters the system through participation, not purchase. It flows between creators, venues, and members as activity occurs, then exits into operational rails where appropriate.

Founding Thesis

Cocktail culture is built by individuals — bartenders, creators, and operators — yet ownership of that culture has historically accrued elsewhere.

Recipes travel faster than the people who create them. Venues profit from differentiation. Guests participate in culture without participating in its upside.

Crypto Cocktail Club introduces ownership primitives to hospitality without disrupting what already works — treating cocktails as intellectual property, experiences as access-controlled systems, and venues as network participants.

The protocol links digital access with real-world hospitality — allowing value to circulate between creators, venues, and members without financial abstraction.

Roadmap Philosophy

Crypto Cocktail Club is built iteratively, alongside venues and creators, not ahead of them.

Features are introduced only when they reduce friction, unlock ownership, or improve real-world operations. Speculation-first mechanics are deliberately excluded.

The protocol evolves at the pace of hospitality—not crypto cycles.
