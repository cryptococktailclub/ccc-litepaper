Crypto Cocktail Club
Bar Litepaper

Protocol Whitepaper

Version 1.1 · January 2026 · Authored by Michael Newton, Founder & CEO

1. Executive Summary

Crypto Cocktail Club (CCC) is an event-driven hospitality protocol designed to modernize how cocktail culture, venues, and creators coordinate value without turning bars into crypto businesses.

CCC introduces a non-financial on-chain protocol using NFTs for access and redeemable experiences while settling venues and creators off-chain like brand activations.

2. Problem Statement

Hospitality is culturally rich but economically misaligned. Bartenders create intellectual property but rarely receive royalties. Venues operate on thin margins with limited upside. Brand activations are opaque and difficult to reconcile, while crypto payment models introduce regulatory and UX friction.

3. Design Principles

  • Bars never accept crypto from guests
  • Creators are paid immediately
  • Venues are insulated from volatility
  • No on-chain payments or swaps
  • Settlement mirrors brand activations
  • Compliance enforced by architectural absence

4. Protocol Overview

  • Membership NFTs (ERC-1155)
  • Cocktail NFTs (ERC-721)
  • TIPZ engagement utility (restricted ERC-20)
  • EscrowVault for deferred funds
  • Off-chain CCC treasury settlement

5. Cocktail NFT Economics

Primary Mint Distribution

  • 90% → Venue providing service
  • 3% → Cocktail creator (paid immediately)
  • 5% → Bartender TIP pool (escrowed)
  • 2% → CCC protocol fee

Secondary Market

  • 5% resale royalty → CCC protocol only

6. Escrow & Settlement Model

ETH is routed deterministically on-chain and held in escrow only when required. All ETH-to-stablecoin or fiat conversion occurs off-chain under CCC treasury operations.

7. Compliance Posture

  • No on-chain swaps or oracles
  • No stablecoin custody
  • No consumer cash-out mechanisms
  • Venues never interact with crypto directly

8. Conclusion

CCC provides a pragmatic bridge between hospitality and blockchain by centralizing complexity at the protocol layer while preserving operator simplicity and compliance.

9. Phased Deployment Strategy

CCC is deployed through phased execution: pilot activations, protocol hardening with escrow and audits, and network expansion across venues and markets. Phases are milestones, not guarantees.

10. Risks & Design Tradeoffs

Risks include adoption challenges, operational centralization in early phases, NFT market volatility, regulatory interpretation, and execution dependencies. Architectural restraint limits downside exposure.

11. Security & Audit Posture

Security is enforced via surface-area minimization. No swaps, oracles, or speculative custody. Third-party audits focus on allocation correctness and access control.

12. Governance & Control Model

CCC operates under centralized governance in v1.1. No DAO or token-based voting is employed. Governance may evolve after sustained product-market fit.

13. Measuring Protocol Success

Success is measured through real-world execution: creator participation, redemption completion, venue retention, settlement accuracy, TIP pool distribution, and brand activation performance.

TIPZ Tokenomics

TIPZ is a utility settlement token designed to route value across the Crypto Cocktail Club (CCC) ecosystem at the moment of hospitality activity. It functions as a programmable gratuity and settlement unit, enabling transparent distribution of value to venues, bartenders, creators, and the CCC protocol—without requiring venues or staff to assume cryptocurrency custody or market risk.

TIPZ is not a speculative asset. It is usage-driven, deflationary by design, and tightly coupled to real-world consumption events.

Design Principles

  • Usage-only emission: TIPZ enters circulation exclusively through verified hospitality activity.
  • No venue crypto exposure: Venues never custody TIPZ.
  • Immediate labor settlement: Bartenders are never required to hold TIPZ.
  • Fixed supply: No inflation or discretionary minting.
  • On-chain enforcement: Emissions, splits, conversions, and burns are auditable.

Token Supply

Total Supply: 1,000,000,000 TIPZ (fixed)

TIPZ has no rebasing, no inflation, and no post-genesis minting beyond predefined emission logic.

Emission Mechanics

TIPZ is emitted only when a verified hospitality action occurs, including:

  • Cocktail NFT redemption
  • Membership utility redemption
  • Confirmed event attendance
  • Brand-sponsored activations

Each emission requires a one-time activity proof, preventing replay or double issuance. No staking, idle farming, or time-based emissions exist.

Value Routing & Splits

Upon emission, TIPZ is routed to a settlement contract and split according to protocol-enforced parameters.

Recipient Allocation
Venue (settled in stablecoin or fiat) 85–90%
Bartender / Creator Tip Pool 5–10%
CCC Protocol Fee 3–5%

Percentages may vary by activation but are enforced on-chain and disclosed in advance.

Settlement Models

Model A — On-Chain Stablecoin Settlement (Default)

  • TIPZ is converted into stablecoin at time of redemption
  • Bartenders receive stablecoin, not TIPZ
  • No crypto custody required for staff
  • Claim-based or automatic payout supported

Model B — Off-Chain Fiat Settlement (Optional)

  • TIPZ is converted into stablecoin
  • Amounts are logged on-chain for auditability
  • CCC settles payments to bartenders in fiat via payroll or ACH

Burn Mechanics

TIPZ is permanently removed from circulation when:

  • Converted to stablecoin for settlement
  • Used for protocol fees
  • Redeemed for non-recyclable perks

As CCC activity increases, circulating TIPZ supply decreases, introducing usage-driven deflation.

Relationship to Membership NFTs

Membership NFTs gate access and participation. TIPZ monetizes activity within that access. Higher membership tiers unlock enhanced earning multipliers and expanded utility.

What TIPZ Is Not

  • Not equity
  • Not profit-sharing
  • Not a dividend or yield token
  • Not required for access to CCC

TIPZ is a settlement and incentive mechanism, not a financial instrument.
