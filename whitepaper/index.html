1. Executive Summary

Crypto Cocktail Club (CCC) is an event-driven hospitality protocol designed to modernize how cocktail culture, venues, and creators coordinate value—without turning bars into crypto businesses.

CCC introduces a non-financial on-chain protocol that uses NFTs for access and redeemable experiences while settling venues and creators off-chain like brand activations.

2. Problem Statement

  • Bartenders create intellectual property but rarely receive royalties
  • Venues operate on thin margins with limited upside
  • Brand activations are opaque and difficult to reconcile
  • Crypto payment models introduce regulatory and UX friction

3. Design Principles

  • Bars never accept crypto from guests
  • Creators are paid immediately
  • Venues are insulated from volatility
  • No on-chain payments or swaps
  • Settlement mirrors brand activations
  • Compliance enforced by architectural absence

4. Protocol Overview

  • Membership NFTs (ERC-1155)
  • Cocktail NFTs (ERC-721)
  • TIPZ engagement utility (restricted ERC-20)
  • EscrowVault for deferred funds
  • Off-chain CCC treasury settlement

5. Cocktail NFT Economics

Primary Mint Distribution (100%)

  • 90% → Venue providing service
  • 3% → Cocktail creator (paid immediately)
  • 5% → Bartender TIP pool (escrowed)
  • 2% → CCC protocol fee

Secondary Market

  • 5% resale royalty → CCC protocol (EIP-2981)
  • No creator or venue resale royalties

6. Escrow & Settlement Model

ETH is routed deterministically on-chain and held in escrow only when required. All ETH-to-stablecoin or fiat conversion occurs off-chain under CCC treasury operations.

7. Compliance Posture

  • No on-chain swaps or oracles
  • No stablecoin custody
  • No consumer cash-out mechanisms
  • Venues never interact with crypto

8. Conclusion

Crypto Cocktail Club provides a pragmatic bridge between hospitality and blockchain by centralizing complexity at the protocol layer while preserving operator simplicity and compliance.

9. Phased Deployment Strategy

Crypto Cocktail Club is being deployed deliberately through phased execution rather than speculative scale.

Each phase validates operational assumptions, hardens protocol infrastructure, and aligns incentives across creators, venues, and partners.

  • Phase I — Pilot Activations: Limited creator cohort, select venues, manual settlement, UX validation.
  • Phase II — Protocol Hardening: EscrowVault live, automated routing, external audit, expanded brand activations.
  • Phase III — Network Expansion: Broader venue onboarding, membership scaling, international rollout.

Phases are operational milestones, not timelines or guarantees.

10. Risks & Design Tradeoffs

  • Adoption Risk: Network effects may develop slower than anticipated.
  • Operational Centralization: Early treasury operations are centralized.
  • Market Dynamics: Secondary NFT markets may be thin or volatile.
  • Regulatory Interpretation: Jurisdictional rules may evolve.
  • Execution Risk: Technical or partnership dependencies may impact rollout.

Architectural restraint limits downside exposure should these risks materialize.

11. Security & Audit Posture

Security is enforced through surface-area minimization and deterministic logic.

  • No swaps, oracles, or price feeds
  • No stablecoin custody
  • No consumer speculative balances
  • Deterministic allocation routing

Third-party audits are intended prior to expanded deployment, focusing on allocation correctness and access control.

12. Governance & Control Model

CCC operates under centralized governance during early phases by design.

No DAO or token-based governance is employed in Version 1.1. Ownership of NFTs or TIPZ confers no governance rights.

Governance may evolve after sustained product-market fit is achieved.

13. Measuring Protocol Success

  • Active creators minting Cocktail NFTs
  • Redemption completion rate
  • Repeat participation by venues
  • Settlement accuracy and timeliness
  • TIP pool distribution effectiveness
  • Brand activation retention

Success is measured through real-world execution, not speculative metrics.
