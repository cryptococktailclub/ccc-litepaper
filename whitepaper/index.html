1. Executive Summary

Crypto Cocktail Club (CCC) is a hospitality protocol designed to introduce ownership, attribution, and incentive coordination into cocktail culture without turning bars into crypto businesses.

The protocol uses NFTs for access and redeemable experiences while settling venues and creators off-chain, mirroring traditional brand activations.

2. Problem Statement

Cocktail culture creates enduring economic and cultural value, yet ownership remains informal and fragmented. Bartenders rarely participate in upside, venues operate on thin margins, and brand activations remain opaque.

3. Design Principles

  • No crypto at point of sale
  • Immediate creator compensation
  • Venue insulation from volatility
  • No on-chain swaps or cash-out mechanics
  • Compliance enforced by architectural absence

4. Protocol Overview

  • Membership NFTs (ERC-1155)
  • Cocktail NFTs (ERC-721)
  • TIPZ incentive coordination token
  • EscrowVault for deferred settlement
  • Off-chain CCC treasury operations

5. Cocktail NFT Economics

Primary Mint Allocation

  • 90% → Venue providing service
  • 3% → Cocktail creator (immediate)
  • 5% → Bartender TIP pool (escrowed)
  • 2% → CCC protocol fee

Secondary Market

  • 5% royalty → CCC protocol (EIP-2981)

6. Escrow & Settlement

Funds are escrowed on-chain only when necessary. All ETH-to-fiat or stablecoin conversion occurs off-chain under CCC treasury operations.

7. Compliance Posture

CCC does not custody user funds, process consumer payments, or expose venues to crypto. The protocol is intentionally constrained to minimize regulatory risk.

8. Conclusion

Crypto Cocktail Club introduces ownership and incentive alignment into hospitality while preserving simplicity, compliance, and operator familiarity.
