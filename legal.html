Legal & Protocol Framework

Investor & Partner Reference Document

1. Executive Summary

Crypto Cocktail Club (“CCC”) operates a cultural IP registry and activation protocol for cocktail recipes. CCC NFTs are not collectibles and are not scarcity-based assets. Each recipe is minted once as a canonical registry record and is designed for broad, repeated real-world use across participating venues and activations.

Value accrues through usage-based activation, transferable economic rights, and protocol fees—not speculation.

2. Canonical NFT Model

Each cocktail recipe is minted once as a non-fungible token representing the canonical registry entry for that recipe. There are no editions, no supply constraints, and no artificial scarcity.

3. Separation of Rights

  • Authorship: Permanent creator attribution (non-transferable)
  • Usage: Open activation by participating venues
  • Economic Rights: Transferable monetization rights

4. Usage & Activation Model

Venues do not purchase or own recipes. They activate registered recipes through the CCC protocol. Each activation is logged and may trigger settlement to the current economic rights holder.

5. Economic Rights Ownership Marketplace

Creators may optionally sell the economic rights associated with a recipe via the CCC marketplace. A sale transfers future activation revenue and monetization control but does not transfer authorship or attribution.

Marketplace Fee

Crypto Cocktail Club takes a flat 10% fee on all rights ownership sales and resales.

6. Mint Fee

Each recipe mint carries a one-time publishing fee that covers secure publishing, permanent archival, and registry indexing. CCC sponsors all underlying blockchain costs.

7. Creator Originality Attestation

By minting a recipe, creators attest that the recipe is their original creation or that they hold the legal right to publish it. CCC relies on attestations and enforces them through its dispute framework.

8. Dispute & Takedown Framework

CCC recognizes disputes related to authorship, fraud, legal claims, or technical errors. CCC does not adjudicate creative merit or revenue performance.

9. Venue Liability Disclaimer

Venues activating recipes are responsible for service execution, safety, and regulatory compliance. CCC does not sell alcohol or control venue operations.

10. Buyer Risk Disclosure

Purchasing economic rights involves commercial risk. CCC does not guarantee usage frequency, revenue outcomes, or market demand. Rights ownership is not equity or a security.

11. Brand Collaboration Carve-Outs

Recipes developed under brand collaborations may include modified monetization or activation rules, which must be disclosed at registration. Authorship attribution remains permanent.

12. Regulatory Posture

CCC operates as a cultural IP registry, rights marketplace, and settlement coordinator. CCC does not provide investment products or custody alcohol-related revenue.

13. Governing Law

These terms are governed by the laws of the State of New York, with exclusive jurisdiction in New York County courts.

14. Canonical Summary

Crypto Cocktail Club is a cultural IP registry and activation protocol where cocktail recipes are minted once, used broadly, monetized through real-world activation, and optionally traded as economic rights—with CCC taking a flat 10% marketplace fee.
