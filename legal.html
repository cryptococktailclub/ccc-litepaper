Legal & Protocol Framework

Rights architecture, attestations, and regulatory posture

1. Executive Summary

Crypto Cocktail Club (“CCC”) operates a cultural IP registry and activation protocol for cocktail recipes. The protocol establishes authorship, coordinates real-world usage, and enables optional transfer of economic rights without introducing speculative financial instruments or operational control over alcohol service.

2. Protocol Positioning & Legal Nature

CCC is not a financial product, investment vehicle, or securities offering. The protocol does not sell alcohol, custody consumer funds, provide payment services, or guarantee revenue outcomes.

NFTs and rights recorded within the protocol represent functional cultural IP interests and usage coordination mechanisms, not equity, profit-sharing instruments, or investment contracts.

3. Canonical NFT Model

Each cocktail recipe is minted once as a canonical registry record. There are no editions, no artificial scarcity mechanics, and no supply-driven speculative design.

4. Separation of Rights

  • Authorship: Permanent, non-transferable attribution to the creator
  • Usage: Open activation by participating venues under protocol rules
  • Economic Rights: Optional, transferable rights to future protocol-mediated monetization

5. Economic Rights Sales & Marketplace Fees

Creators may optionally sell the economic rights associated with a recipe through the CCC marketplace. Such a sale transfers future monetization rights within the protocol only.

Authorship, attribution, and historical provenance are never transferred.

CCC retains a flat 10% fee on all economic rights sales and resales, without tiering or exceptions.

6. Creator Originality Attestation

By minting a recipe, creators attest that the recipe is their original creation or that they possess lawful rights to publish it. Attestations are timestamped, persistent, and survive any transfer of economic rights.

7. Dispute & Takedown Policy

CCC recognizes disputes limited to authorship claims, fraud, misrepresentation, or valid legal orders. CCC does not adjudicate creative merit or commercial performance.

Remedies may include registry annotation, suspension of economic rights, marketplace delisting, or limited activation takedown. Historical records are never deleted.

8. Venue-Side Activation Liability Disclaimer

Venues are solely responsible for cocktail preparation, service execution, staff conduct, ingredient sourcing, and compliance with applicable alcohol, health, and labor regulations.

CCC does not supervise, control, or participate in venue operations and bears no liability for service outcomes.

9. Buyer Risk Disclosure

Purchasing economic rights involves commercial risk. CCC does not guarantee activation frequency, revenue generation, or market demand.

Economic rights are not equity interests, securities, or profit guarantees. Buyers assume all market and operational risk.

10. Brand Collaboration Carve-Outs

Recipes developed under brand collaborations may include modified economic arrangements, usage constraints, or activation scopes. Any such carve-outs are disclosed at registration and do not affect authorship permanence.

11. Regulatory Posture

CCC is architected to minimize regulatory exposure through structural restraint: no on-chain payments, no custody, no consumer cash-out, and no venue crypto interaction.

12. Governing Law & Jurisdiction

All protocol participation, marketplace activity, and disputes are governed by the laws of the State of New York, with exclusive jurisdiction in New York County courts.
